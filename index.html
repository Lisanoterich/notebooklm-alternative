Frequently Asked Questions

Is NoteRich really a NotebookLM alternative?

Yes — if your priority is AI-powered document Q&A. Both tools let you import documents and ask natural language questions. NoteRich adds privacy (local storage), a full rich-text editor, larger file support, and works offline. If you use NotebookLM for research and writing, NoteRich covers those workflows and more.

Where does my data go when I use NoteRich?

Your data stays on your device. Period. NoteRich stores everything in your browser's local storage (IndexedDB). For AI features, only anonymized, minimal context snippets are temporarily sent — and never stored. NoteRich does not train on your data.

Does NoteRich work offline?

Yes. All document indexing and search runs locally in your browser via Web Workers. You can search your knowledge base without an internet connection. AI Q&A requires connectivity for the synthesis step, but your data is never uploaded — only anonymized context is sent.

How many documents can I import?

NoteRich supports up to 7,000,000+ characters per note and 20,000+ notes per workspace. That's orders of magnitude more than NotebookLM's 50-source limit. You can create multiple workspaces for different projects.

Can I use NoteRich on my phone?

Yes. NoteRich is a PWA (Progressive Web App) — it works on any device with a modern browser. The responsive UI adapts to mobile screens. Open noterich.com on your phone and start using it.

Is NoteRich free?

NoteRich has a generous free tier: full editor, up to 5 workspaces, basic AI search, local storage. The Pro plan ($39.90/year) adds unlimited workspaces, large knowledge base RAG, encrypted exports, PDF export, password-protected sharing, and priority support.
